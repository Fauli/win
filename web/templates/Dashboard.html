Dashboard

This site allows you to see correlations between various sources in regards to a special topic.

Currently this is implemented for Bitcoins, as the whole currency information are available online. At the moment the following sources are available:

  • Bitcoin: Daily Average of Bitcoin Trades, based on Data from https://bitcoinaverage.com/
  • Google: Amount of Google Requests in Percent of Max-Search Volume during the Timeframe. Data older than 3 Monet is granulated twice a month.
  • Simple Twitter Sentiment Analyser: Daily average of the positive/negative sentiment of 1000 daily tweets about the subject. Assigns a sentiment score to a tweet based on the AFINN-111 Wordset. (contains ~ 2500 words)
  • Advanced Twitter Sentiment Analyser: Daily average of the positive/negative sentiment of 1000 daily tweets about the subject. Assings a sentiment score to a tweet based on the SentiWordNet Wordset. (contains > 115000 words & word relationships)

Improvements:

  • Due to limited time, the scraping of the Twitterdata was limited to 1000 Tweets daily. A higher amount of Tweets would increase the accuracy of our Sentiment Analysis.
  • The accuracy of the sentiment analysis at the moment is not high enough to generate sufficient correlation. A higher correlaction could definitelly be achived, besides with more twitter data, with an integration of correlation analysis between words und more twitter/market specific wordsets.
